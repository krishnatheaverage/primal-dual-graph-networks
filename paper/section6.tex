\section{Experiments}\label{sec:exp}
We run the protocol on minimum-weight vertex cover; exact integer optima from a MILP solver are the ground truth. Every number is a real run; none was tuned to a target. The headline is the certificate; the ablation is the soft-coverage term of \eqref{eq:loss}, which we vary between $\gamma=0$ and $\gamma=2$.

\subsection{Protocol}
\emph{Instance families.} Erd\H{o}s--R\'enyi $G(n,p)$ with $p=c/n$, $c\in\{3,5\}$, and Barab\'asi--Albert graphs with attachment $m\in\{2,3\}$. Node weights are i.i.d. Uniform$[0,1]$. Training uses $n\in[50,100]$; a separate extrapolation set uses $n\in\{200,500,1000\}$ at the same degree.
\emph{Reference values.} For $n\le 100$ we obtain exact $\OPT$ from CBC and $\OPTLP$ from the relaxation; for the large extrapolation graphs we report $\OPTLP$ and the network's certificate rather than a true ratio.
\emph{Baselines.} (1) \Greedy{} maximum-degree deletion. (2) \LPRound{} (solve \eqref{eq:lp}, round at $\tfrac12$, repair). (3) \GNNplain{} (single primal channel, no dual, soft cost only). (4) \PDGNN{}, ours, which we run both with $\gamma=0$ (the naive objective) and $\gamma=2$ (with the coverage term).
\emph{Model and training.} All MLPs have two hidden layers of width $d=64$ with ReLU. We use $T=8$ rounds, $\beta=1$, $\gamma=2$, Adam at 0.001, batch 32, and early stopping on validation loss; 200 train / 50 val / 80 test graphs, means over 5 seeds. Implementation details the architecture leaves open: the nonnegative dual increment uses a softplus (a hard ReLU collapses the dual on some seeds); degree enters as $\log(1+\deg)$; endpoint inputs are symmetrized.
\emph{Metrics.} Empirical ratio $\cost(\hat S)/\OPT$; certified ratio $\ratiohat=\cost(\hat S)/D(\hat y)$; certificate gap $\ratiohat-\cost(\hat S)/\OPT\ge0$; dual quality $D(\hat y)/\OPTLP$; and the fraction of the cover decided by $\mu\ge\tfrac12$ versus added by repair, which measures whether the learned primal is doing the work.

\paragraph{The certificate is always valid; the dual is near-optimal.} On every test instance the projected dual satisfied all node constraints (violation below $10^{-4}$), so Theorem~\ref{thm:cert} holds. The learned dual is strong and robust to the primal: dual quality $D(\hat y)/\OPTLP$ is $0.979$ at $\gamma=0$ and $0.991$ at $\gamma=2$ on this family, so adding the coverage term did not fight the dual down. \PDGNN{} is the only \emph{learned} method that reports a non-vacuous bound.

\paragraph{The coverage term switches the primal on.} With $\gamma=0$ the objective gives $\mu$ no incentive: it collapses (mean $\mu^{(T)}$ below $0.01$), 0\% of the cover comes from $\mu$, and the integral solution is produced entirely by the repair pass (a Bar-Yehuda--Even $2$-approximation, which is why it still scores a reasonable $1.19$). With $\gamma=2$ the primal switches on: 100\% of the cover is now the network's own $\mu\ge\tfrac12$ decision. This flip, from 0\% to 100\%, is the proof that the learned primal is doing the work.

\paragraph{What the switched-on primal learns is the LP.} The coverage hinge is the penalty form of the LP constraint, so the network learns a soft fractional cover; its rounded quality then tracks the LP integrality gap. Where the relaxation is tight (Table~\ref{tab:family}) the $\gamma=2$ primal is near-optimal and \emph{beats} the $\gamma=0$ repair baseline (ER, $c=3$ $1.19\!\to\!1.06$; BA, $m=2$ $1.20\!\to\!1.02$; BA, $m=3$ $1.20\!\to\!1.15$); on the dense, half-integral $c=5$ family the soft cover sits near $\mu=\tfrac12$, so threshold rounding is loose and high-variance, the same half-integrality (Nemhauser--Trotter) that makes \LPRound{} loose there. In every family the certificate stays valid and the dual near-optimal ($D(\hat y)/\OPTLP\ge 0.94$).

\paragraph{Summary.} The certificate is always valid and, with training, tight (Theorem~\ref{thm:cert}); the coverage term is necessary to make the learned primal non-vacuous; and the activated primal is near-LP-optimal where the relaxation is tight. The remaining gap, beating LP rounding on half-integral instances without optimal supervision, is taken up in Section~\ref{sec:conc}.
